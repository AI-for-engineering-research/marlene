Spotlight · Summer 2026

Sensor overlay

Combining observations from multiple platforms — GOES ABI, VIIRS, ground cameras, lidar, and radiosondes — into a combined and interactive sensor overlay tool.

No single sensor captures the full picture of a contrail formation an persistence. Geostationary satellites offer temporal continuity; low-Earth orbiters provide sharper spatial detail over still large spatial coverage; ground cameras give a direct visual reference at time of formation; lidars resolve the vertical structure and ice crystal properties; and radiosondes characterize the atmospheric conditions that govern persistence. Overlaying these sources onto a shared coordinate frame makes it possible to identify the same contrail across instruments and link its observable properties to the underlying meteorology. As a starting point, this tool shows the availability of data form various sensors over the MIT Sky Camera for the past seven days.

Marlene V. Euchenhofer · MIT Laboratory for Aviation and the Environment · 2026
